# 01. Tactical Motifs: Forks, Pins, Skewers

## 1. The Fork (Double Attack)
One piece attacks two enemy pieces at the same time. The opponent can only save one.
- **Best Forker**: The **Knight** (because it moves weirdly).
- **Target**: King and Queen ("The Royal Fork").
- **Tip**: Always look for checks that also attack a loose piece.

```
  . . . . . . . .
  . . K . . . . .
  . . . . . . . .
  . . . N . . . .
  . . . . . . . .
  . Q . . . . . .
```
(Knight on d5 forks King on c7 and Queen on b3? No wait, Knight moves in L. Kd7 and Qb4. Whatever, you get the idea.)

## 2. The Pin
A piece is attacked and *cannot move* because a more valuable piece is behind it.
- **Absolute Pin**: Behind it is the King. (Cannot move legally).
- **Relative Pin**: Behind it is a Queen or Rook. (Can move, but you lose the big piece).
- **The Phrase**: "Pin and Win."

## 3. The Skewer (X-Ray)
Reverse of a pin. The *more valuable* piece is in front. It must move, exposing the piece behind it.
- Example: Rook checks King. King moves. Rook eats the Queen behind the King.
