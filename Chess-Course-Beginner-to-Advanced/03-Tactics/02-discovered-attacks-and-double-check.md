# 02. Discovered Attacks and Double Check

## Discovered Attack
You move a piece, and it *uncovers* an attack from a piece behind it (like a Bishop or Rook).
- It is powerful because the moving piece can go *anywhere* (even freely taking stuff) because the opponent has to deal with the discovered threat.

## Discovered Check
The uncovered attack is a CHECK.
- Extremely dangerous. The opponent MUST respond to the check.

## Double Check
You move a piece to give check, AND the piece you uncovered ALSO gives check.
- **The Most Powerful Move in Chess.**
- The King **MUST MOVE**.
- He cannot block (because two pieces are checking).
- He cannot capture (unless the King captures one, but the other is still there—wait, no, he just can't capturing is usually not possible if both are checking from distance, or if one is close, the other protects... basically, checking two pieces at once usually means the King must run).

## Key Ideas
- Always line up your Rooks vs their Queen/King, even if pawns are in the way. The pawns will move eventually!
