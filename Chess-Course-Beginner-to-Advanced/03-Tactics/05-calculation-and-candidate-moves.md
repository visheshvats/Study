# 05. Calculation and Candidate Moves

## What is Calculation?
Visualizing moves in your head without moving the pieces.
- **Rule**: "If I go there, he goes there, then I go there..."

## Candidate Moves
Before you calculate deeply, list the options.
- Don't just look at the first move you see.
- **Kotov's Tree**: Look for 3-4 possible moves ("Candidates"). Calculate each one to the end of the forceful sequence.

## The Checklist for Candidate Moves
1.  **Checks**: Always look at checks first. (They force a response).
2.  **Captures**: Look at every capture.
3.  **Threats**: Moves that create a mate threat or attack a Queen.

## Validation
Once you find a move, do a **Blunder Check**.
"If I play this, does he have a mate in 1? Or a Knight fork?"

## Drills
1.  Pick a puzzle. Don't move until you see the solution *fully* in your head.
2.  "Sit on your hands" (literally) to prevent impulsive moves.
