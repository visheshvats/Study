# Daily Routine for Improvement

Results come from consistency, not intensity. 30 minutes a day is better than 5 hours once a week.

## The 30-Minute "Balanced Diet"
If you have limited time, split it like this:

1.  **Tactics (10 mins)**
    *   Solve puzzles.
    *   Focus on accuracy first, speed second.
    *   If you fail a puzzle, retry it until you understand *why*.

2.  **Study/Learn (10 mins)**
    *   Read one section of this course.
    *   OR review an endgame position.
    *   OR watch an educational video (checking the board, not just watching).

3.  **Play & Review (10 mins)**
    *   This is tight for playing. If you only have 10 mins left, play **Rapid (10+0 or 15+10)**? No. Analysis paralysis.
    *   *Correction*: If you only have 30 mins total, alternate days:
        *   **Day A**: Tactics (15m) + Study (15m)
        *   **Day B**: Play one game (20m) + Quick Review (10m)

## The 60-Minute "Serious Student"
1.  **Warm-up Tactics (10 mins)**: Simple patterns.
2.  **Serious Calculation (15 mins)**: 2-3 hard puzzles. Calculate all variations before moving.
3.  **Course Work (20 mins)**: Read chapters, take notes.
4.  **Play (15 mins)**: Play one rapid game.
5.  **Review (??)**: Do you have time? If not, save the review for tomorrow's "Course Work" slot.

## Important Rules
*   **Don't play Bullet/Blitz constantly.** It builds bad habits. Stick to 10+5 (10 mins + 5 sec increment) or slower.
*   **Analyze every game.** Even if it's just for 2 minutes. "Where did I leave the opening book?", "Where did I blunder?".
*   **Physical Board.** Occasionally use a real board to improve 3D visualization.
