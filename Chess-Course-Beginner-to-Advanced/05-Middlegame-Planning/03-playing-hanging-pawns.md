# 03. Playing Hanging Pawns

Two pawns (c and d) side by side, separated from others.
Ex: Pawns on c4, d4.

## Strengths
- Control central squares (b5, c5, d5, e5).
- Can transform into a Passed Pawn or IQP.

## Weaknesses
- If they are forced to advance, they leave holes behind.
- They are targets for Rooks.

## Plan
- **With them**: Keep them mobile. Support with Rooks.
- **Against them**: Force one to move (e.g., provoke d4-d5), then blockade the square in front/behind.

## Drills
1.  Look up "Hanging Pawns Karpov" games. He was a master of playing against them.
