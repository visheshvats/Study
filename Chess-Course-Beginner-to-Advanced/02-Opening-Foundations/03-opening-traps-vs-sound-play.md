# 03. Opening Traps vs Sound Play

## Hope Chess
"Hope Chess" is playing a bad move hoping your opponent falls for a trap.
- **Bad Trap**: Scholar's Mate attempts (`1. e4 e5 2. Qh5?!`). If Black defends correctly, White has a misplaced Queen and bad development.
- **Good Trap**: A trap that arises from *good* moves. If they fall for it, you win. If not, you still have a good position.

## Common Traps to Know (So you don't die)

### 1. The Fishing Pole (Spanish)
White plays Bg5. Black plays ...h6. White plays Bh4. Black plays ...g5. White plays Bg3. Black plays h5! (Threatening h4 to trap bishop).

### 2. Legal's Mate (Italian)
Sacrificing the Queen for a mate with minor pieces.
`1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 d6 3. Bc4 Bg4 4. Nc3 g6? 5. Nxe5! Bxd1?? 6. Bxf7+ Ke7 7. Nd5#`

### 3. Fried Liver Attack
`1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Nf6 4. Ng5 d5 5. exd5 Nxd5 6. Nxf7!`
Very dangerous for Black. Black should have played `5... Na5` (Polerio Defense).

## Philosophy
- Don't play specific "Trap Openings" (e.g., Stafford Gambit) exclusively. They stop working as you get better.
- Play sound openings, and learn the tactical patterns that appear *within* them.
