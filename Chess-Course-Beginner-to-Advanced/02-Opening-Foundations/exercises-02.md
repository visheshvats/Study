# Module 02 Exercises

## Part A: Principles
1.  What are the three goals of the opening?
2.  Why is `1. h4` usually considered a bad opening move?
3.  When should you usually castle? (Move range)
4.  Why is `3... a6` usually a waste of time in the opening unless it has a specific purpose?

## Part B: Repertoire Knowledge
5.  In the Italian Game (`1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4`), which square does the Bishop attack?
6.  If you are Black and White plays `1. d4`, what is the solid reply recommended in this course?
7.  If White plays `1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Nf6`, what is this defense called?

## Part C: Scenarios
8.  **Position**: White has played 1.e4, 2.Qh5, 3.Bc4. (Scholar's Mate attempt).
    Black to move. What is the best move to stop mate and attack the Queen?
    a) ...Ke7
    b) ...g6
    c) ...Nf6

9.  **Position**: It's move 7. You have moved your Knight 3 times to eat a pawn. Your opponent has developed 3 pieces and castled. Who is better?

10. **Trap Check**:
    White plays `1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 d6 3. Bc4 Bg4 4. Nc3 h6?`.
    Is Black safe from Legal's Mate now? (Hint: Does the Queen sacrifice still work if h6 prevents Ng5?)
