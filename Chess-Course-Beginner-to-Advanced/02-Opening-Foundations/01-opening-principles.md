# 01. Opening Principles

The goal of the opening is NOT to win immediately (unless opponent blunders).
The goal is to reach a playable middlegame with:
1.  Control of the Center.
2.  Active Pieces.
3.  A Safe King.

## The Three Golden Rules

### 1. Control the Center
The center is e4, d4, e5, d5.
- Imagine the board as a hill. The top is the center. Pieces there can see everything.
- **Best First Moves**: `1. e4` or `1. d4`. Why? They occupy the center and open lines for Bishops/Queen.

### 2. Develop Your Pieces
"Development" means getting pieces off the back rank.
- **Knights before Bishops**: Usually better because Knights are short-range and know where they belong (f3, c3, f6, c6).
- **Don't move the same piece twice**: Unless attacked. Developing a new piece is usually better than moving an old one again.
- **Don't bring the Queen out too early**: She will get chased by enemy Knights/Bishops, and you will lose time.

### 3. King Safety
- **Castle Early**: Usually within the first 10 moves.
- Don't move the pawns in front of your Castled King unnecessarily.

## Common Mistakes
- `1. h4?`: Does nothing for the center, develops nothing.
- "Pawn Hunting": Taking a dangerous "poisoned" pawn with your Queen while your King is in the center.

## Drills
1.  Play 5 games where you make a pact: "I will not move a piece a second time until I have castled."
