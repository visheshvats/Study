# 02. Developing a Repertoire

A repertoire is a set of openings you specialize in. You don't need to know everything, just *something* for every common response.

## For Beginners: "System" vs "Theory"
- **Theory Heavy**: Sicilian Najdorf, King's Indian Defense. (Avoid these for now. Too much memorization).
- **System Based**: London System, Colle System, King's Indian Attack. (Good! Same setup almost every game).
- **Principled**: Italian Game, Scotch Game. (Best! Teaches you how to play chess).

## Recommended Approach
Don't memorize 20 moves deep. Memorize the **plans**.

### As White
- Play `1. e4`.
- If Black plays `1... e5`: Go for the **Italian Game** (2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4).
- If Black plays `1... c5` (Sicilian) or others: Play sensible developing moves (Nf3, d4, Nc3).

### As Black
- Against `1. e4`: Play `1... e5`. (Open Games).
- Against `1. d4`: Play `1... d5`. (Queen's Gambit Declined options).

## The "Checklist" Approach
When learning a new opening, ask:
1.  Where do my Knights go?
2.  Where do my Bishops go?
3.  What is the "pawn break" (e.g., c5? f5? d5?)?

## Drills
1.  Pick ONE opening for White. Watch a 10-minute Youtube video on it.
2.  Play it in 5 blitz games.
