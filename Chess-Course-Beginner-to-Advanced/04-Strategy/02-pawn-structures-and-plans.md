# 02. Pawn Structures and Plans

Pawns are the soul of chess. They determine where the pieces go.

## Common Structures

### 1. The Pawn Chain
- **Rule**: Attack the base of the chain.
- Ex: White pawns on d4, e5. (Pointing to Kingside). White should attack Kingside. Black should attack d4 or f4 (Base).

### 2. Doubled Pawns
- Two pawns of the same color on the same file.
- **Bad**: They can't defend each other. Hard to move.
- **Good**: They open a file for your Rooks. They control more central squares (sometimes).

### 3. Isolated Pawn (Isolani)
- A pawn with no friendly pawns on adjacent files.
- **Weakness**: Must be defended by pieces.
- **Strength**: Supports piece activity in the center.

## Key Ideas
- "Pawn Breaks": A pawn move that attacks an enemy pawn to open lines (e.g., ...c5 in the French). You *need* pawn breaks to win.
