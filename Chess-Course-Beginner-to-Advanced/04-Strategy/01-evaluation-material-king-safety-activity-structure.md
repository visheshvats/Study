# 01. Evaluation: Material, King Safety, Activity, Structure

To form a plan, you must know who is winning and why.
**Imbalances** are the differences between your position and the opponent's.

## The 4 Main Factors of Evaluation

### 1. Material (The "Count")
- P=1, N=3, B=3, R=5, Q=9.
- Basic math. If you are down a Rook, you are losing unless you have immediate mate.

### 2. King Safety
- A King in the center is a target.
- Damaged pawn shield (e.g., pushed f-pawn) is weak.
- **Rule**: Material doesn't matter if you get checkmated.

### 3. Piece Activity
- "Good pieces" control many squares and attack targets.
- "Bad pieces" are stuck behind pawns or doing nothing.
- **Rule**: An active Knight is better than a passive Rook sometimes.

### 4. Pawn Structure
- **Weaknesses**: Doubled pawns, Isolated pawns, Backward pawns.
- These are long-term problems. (See Lesson 02).

## Drills
1.  Open any game in a book/database. Cover the moves. Guess the evaluation (+/-) based on these 4 factors.
