# 03. Good vs Bad Bishops, Knights, Outposts

## Good vs Bad Bishops
- **Bad Bishop**: Blocked by its own pawns. (e.g., Light-squared Bishop stuck behind pawns on d4/e5/f4).
- **Good Bishop**: Has open diagonals and attacks enemy pawns.
- **Plan**: Trade your Bad Bishop for their Good Knight!

## Knights and Outposts
- **Outpost**: A square that cannot be attacked by enemy pawns. usually supported by your own pawn.
- **The Ideal Knight**: Sitting on an outpost in the center (e.g., d5 or e5), supported by a pawn. It radiates power.
- "An Octopus Knight" (Controls 8 squares inside enemy territory).

## Strategy
1.  Identify your candidate outpost.
2.  Maneuver your Knight there.
3.  Don't let them trade it easily.

## Drills
1.  Place a White Knight on d5 (Pawn on e4 protecting it). Place Black pawns on c7, e7. (No c6/e6 pawn can drive it away). Feel the power.
