# 02. Piece Moves and Captures

## The Pieces

### The Rook (R)
- Moves **horizontally** or **vertically** any number of squares.
- Cannot jump over pieces.
- Powerful piece (Value: 5 points).

### The Bishop (B)
- Moves **diagonally** any number of squares.
- Stays on one color complex (Light-squared bishop vs Dark-squared bishop).
- Value: 3 points.

### The Queen (Q)
- Combined power of Rook + Bishop.
- Moves horizontal, vertical, diagonal.
- Most valuable piece (except King).
- Value: 9 points.

### The Knight (N)
- Moves in an **"L" shape**: 2 squares in one direction, then 1 square perpendicular.
- **Special Ability**: The ONLY piece that can **jump** over others.
- Value: 3 points.

### The Pawn (P)
- Moves **forward only**.
- First move: Can move 1 or 2 squares.
- Later moves: Only 1 square.
- **Captures**: Diagonally forward one square. (Moves straight, eats diagonal).
- Value: 1 point.
- *Promotion*: Reaches the end? Becomes a Q, R, B, or N.

### The King (K)
- Moves 1 square in any direction.
- Cannot move into "check" (danger).
- Value: Infinite (Lost king = Lost game).
- Combat strength: ~4 points in the endgame.

## Captures
- You land on an opponent's piece and replace it.
- You cannot capture your own pieces.

## Key Ideas
- Knights jump.
- Pawns are unique: Move straight, capture diagonal.
- Center control (e4, d4, e5, d5) is crucial for piece mobility.

## Common Mistakes
- Moving a Knight to a square it can't reach (e.g., trying to go 3 squares).
- Forgetting Pawns capture diagonally.

## Drills (10 Mins)
1.  Place a Knight on e4. Find all 8 squares it can jump to.
2.  Place a Bishop on c1. See how many squares it controls. Move a pawn to d2. how many now? (Bad Piece!)
