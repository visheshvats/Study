# 01. Board Setup and Notation

## The Board
The chessboard is an 8x8 grid of 64 squares, alternating light and dark colors.
- **Rule #1**: "White on right". The bottom-right square must be a light square.
- **Files**: Vertical columns (a-h).
- **Ranks**: Horizontal rows (1-8).

```
  8 | r n b q k b n r
  7 | p p p p p p p p
  6 | . . . . . . . .
  5 | . . . . . . . .
  4 | . . . . . . . .
  3 | . . . . . . . .
  2 | P P P P P P P P
  1 | R N B Q K B N R
    +----------------
      a b c d e f g h
```
*Capital letters = White, Small letters = Black*

## setup
- **A & H Files**: Rooks (R)
- **B & G Files**: Knights (N) - "Knights on the rim are dim" (usually)
- **C & F Files**: Bishops (B)
- **D File**: Queen (Q) - "Queen goes on her own color" (White Queen on light square, Black Queen on dark square).
- **E File**: King (K)

## Algebraic Notation
Every square has a name (coordinate): File + Rank.
Ex: The bottom-left square is **a1**. The top-right is **h8**.

### Describing Moves
1.  **Piece Letter** (K, Q, R, B, N). *Note: Pawns have no letter.*
2.  **Destination Square**.
3.  **Examples**:
    - **Nf3**: Knight moves to f3.
    - **e4**: Pawn moves to e4.
    - **Bxc4**: Bishop *captures* on c4. (`x` means capture)
    - **O-O**: Kingside castle.
    - **O-O-O**: Queenside castle.
    - **+**: Check (e.g., Bb5+).
    - **#**: Checkmate (e.g., Qh5#).

## Key Ideas
- Memorize "White on right".
- Queen gets her color.
- Coordinate training is essential for reading books.

## Drills (10 mins)
1.  Go to a blank board. Point to squares and name them randomly (e.g., "Where is c6?").
2.  Set up the board from scratch in under 1 minute correctly.
